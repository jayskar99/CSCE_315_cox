MS150

Every year, for as long as I have been alive, my family volunteers for the MS150. This event is an 150 mile bike race from Houston to Austin to raise money for Multiple Sclerosis. We are in charge of the bike compound at the half way point in La Grange, this is where riders safely keep their bikes overnight. The bike compound is composed of a baseball field where we set up bike racks and signs to designate where to park. Each rider is assigned a rider number which they have on a wristband and a corresponding paper attached to their bike. Our job is to track the inflow and outflow of bikes via their assigned rider numbers. Once the rider's number is entered into our database, we walk them and their bike to the assigned rack according to their number. This is to ensure easy pick up the following day. After the last of the riders enter their bikes at 10PM, we lock up the compound and a local officer guards the only entrance to the field overnight. Waking up bright and early at 5AM, we open the gates and begin to check out bikes ensuring that the rider numbers match and no bikes are being stolen. This checkout process continues until the last riders set out for the day. At that point, we carefully load the remaining bikes onto a truck to be taken to the finish line for anyone who was injured or dropped out of the event. The final step is to break down the bike racks in order to return the field back to a functioning state. This event has been very important in my growth as an individual because it has built a foundation of giving back to my local community. Although my grandma has Multiple Sclerosis, we have always been sure to work hard at this event which raises money to seek a cure.

Aggie Camping

Through my organization, Aggie Camping, I participate in the A&M tradition of The Big Event. Every year, hundereds of student organizations gather teams which are sent out to differnt houses in our local community. The idea is to give back to the place which are school is allowed to thrive. Whatever the resident needs, we help them to complete. Last year, we helped clean a garden bed and get rid of ant hills at a residence. This is such a great time to remind myself to give back to my local community which allows me to thrive, and I am excited to particiapte every year.
